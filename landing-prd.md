# PRD: Landing Page for AI Ingredient Analysis Bot

## 1. Product Overview

The landing page presents a Telegram bot that helps users analyze the ingredient composition of cosmetics, food products, baby food, and pet food from a photo.

The page must explain the core value of the bot, demonstrate how the analysis works, show sample results, build trust in the scoring logic, and convert visitors into bot opens in Telegram.

The landing is based on the approved Figma prototype and should preserve its overall content structure:

- hero section with the main value proposition
- explanation of ingredient analysis by photo
- explanation of ingredient-level safety review
- explanation of the overall product score
- sample recognition result
- step-by-step usage flow
- emotional CTA against low-quality products
- audience segments
- final Telegram-focused conversion block
- footer with category links and disclaimer

## 2. Goal

The main goal of the landing page is to convert visitors into Telegram bot users.

Secondary goals:

- explain what the bot does in plain language
- show that the service is practical and easy to use
- reduce skepticism through visible examples of analysis output
- communicate that the bot works quickly and without subscription friction

## 3. Target Audience

The landing page is intended for users who want to quickly evaluate product composition before purchase or use.

Primary audience segments reflected in the prototype:

- parents checking baby food
- people with problematic or sensitive skin checking cosmetics
- pet owners checking animal food
- health-conscious users checking food and sports nutrition

## 4. Core Value Proposition

The product promise communicated on the page:

- take a photo of the ingredient list
- send it to the Telegram bot
- receive an understandable analysis in around 10 seconds

The user should understand that the bot provides:

- OCR-based ingredient recognition
- explanation of each ingredient
- detection of potentially harmful or undesirable components
- an overall safety score from 0 to 100
- a simple final recommendation in non-technical language

## 5. User Problem

Users struggle to evaluate product compositions because:

- ingredient lists are difficult to read and interpret
- product marketing is often misleading
- independent composition analysis is time-consuming
- many users only need occasional checks and do not want subscriptions

The landing page must make it clear that the bot solves these problems with a fast, accessible Telegram workflow.

## 6. Landing Page Requirements

### 6.1 Hero Section

The first screen must communicate the main promise immediately.

Required content:

- headline about checking product composition quality
- supporting text about cosmetics and food analysis in Telegram in about 10 seconds
- primary CTA to open or check the bot in Telegram
- visual support showing a mobile bot experience

Success criteria for the section:

- the product category is understandable in the first few seconds
- the CTA is visible without scrolling on desktop
- the hero visually establishes trust and product simplicity

### 6.2 Explanation of Analysis by Photo

This section must explain that the user can photograph the ingredient list and upload it to the bot.

Required content:

- short heading about composition recognition from photo
- short supporting explanation of the flow
- supporting visual of the chat or mobile interface

### 6.3 Ingredient-Level Safety Analysis

This block must explain that the bot does not only extract text, but also interprets ingredients one by one.

Required content:

- statement that each component is evaluated
- examples of ingredient callouts such as irritation or risk markers
- visual representation of specific ingredient labels and conclusions

Business purpose:

- show analytical depth
- differentiate the product from plain OCR utilities

### 6.4 Overall Product Score

This section must explain that the bot produces an overall score from 0 to 100 based on the composition.

Required content:

- heading about science-based or data-based score
- example score badges
- short explanatory text that the score reflects composition quality and safety

Business purpose:

- give users a simple final decision aid
- make the output feel actionable, not abstract

### 6.5 Sample Recognition Result

This section must demonstrate what the final output looks like after recognition and analysis.

Required content:

- real-looking ingredient list example
- explanatory narrative about positives and negatives in the composition
- tabbed or segmented examples for different product types

The response preview should communicate:

- raw ingredient text
- plain-language interpretation
- clear reasoning behind the conclusion

### 6.6 Usage Flow Section

This section must show the simple 3-step journey already present in the prototype.

Required steps:

1. photograph the ingredient list on the packaging
2. upload it to the Telegram bot
3. receive a detailed composition review

Business purpose:

- reduce perceived effort
- make the product feel instant and easy

### 6.7 Emotional Conversion Section

This section must address distrust toward low-quality or overmarketed products.

Required content:

- heading about being tired of low-quality product advertising
- statement about independent evaluation without brand influence
- CTA to check composition in Telegram

This block should strengthen motivation before the audience-fit section and final offer.

### 6.8 Audience Segment Section

This section must show who the bot is suitable for.

Required audience cards based on the prototype:

- caring parent
- person with problematic skin
- pet owner
- athlete / health-conscious user

Each card should explain the concrete use case in one short sentence.

### 6.9 Final Conversion Block

The final pre-footer section must reinforce the Telegram-first value proposition.

Required content:

- headline about checking cosmetics and food directly in Telegram
- benefits such as convenience, no subscription, transparent cost, and avoiding expensive plans
- primary CTA to open the bot in Telegram
- supporting visual of the mobile interface

This section should function as the strongest bottom-of-page conversion point.

### 6.10 Footer

The footer must include:

- short product descriptor
- legal/disclaimer line
- category links similar to cosmetics, food, baby food, and pet food

## 7. Functional Requirements

- The page must have at least one primary CTA above the fold and one repeated CTA near the bottom.
- All primary CTAs must lead to the Telegram bot.
- The landing page must be fully responsive for desktop and mobile.
- The content structure must follow the approved prototype ordering.
- The page must support visuals of Telegram/mobile screens and result previews.
- The page must communicate the 0 to 100 score clearly and consistently.
- The page must present the bot as an informational analysis tool, not as medical advice.

## 8. Content Requirements

- Messaging must be simple, direct, and understandable for non-experts.
- The copy must avoid overly technical ingredient terminology unless immediately explained.
- Claims must stay aligned with the actual bot capabilities described in the bot documentation.
- The landing must consistently communicate speed, clarity, and practical usefulness.
- Pricing/value messaging in the final conversion block should preserve the prototype idea of no subscription and low per-use cost.

## 9. UX Requirements

- The page must be easy to scan section by section.
- CTA labels must be unambiguous and action-oriented.
- Key benefits must be understandable without reading long paragraphs.
- Example outputs must look trustworthy and concrete.
- The usage flow must be understandable in a few seconds.
- Mobile experience must preserve readability and conversion emphasis.

## 10. Non-Functional Requirements

- Fast loading on modern desktop and mobile browsers
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Accessible contrast and readable typography
- Stable layout without disruptive visual jumps
- Basic SEO readiness with semantic headings, metadata, and page structure

## 11. Success Metrics

Primary metric:

- click-through rate from landing page to Telegram bot

Secondary metrics:

- scroll depth to sample result and final CTA sections
- CTA click rate by section
- bounce rate from hero section
- engagement with sample result tabs or examples, if implemented interactively

## 12. Constraints

- The PRD is based on the approved Figma prototype structure and content.
- The landing page should not introduce major new sections outside the current prototype without a separate decision.
- The product must be described as a composition analysis assistant and not as a medical diagnostic tool.
- The Telegram-first interaction model is mandatory for the product positioning.

## 13. Out of Scope

- redesign of the landing page information architecture
- full subscription product logic
- user account dashboard
- personal history of analyses
- medical or legal claims beyond ingredient explanation
